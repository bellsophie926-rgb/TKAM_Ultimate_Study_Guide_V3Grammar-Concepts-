Grammar Concepts (四大核心语法考点)

Definition: To change the form of adjectives or adverb to describe things or activities according to a certain comparison level.[cite: 31]
定义：改变形容词或副词的形式，以根据特定的比较级别来描述事物或活动。
Levels (比较级别):
• Positive: Modifies nouns without the need for comparison.[cite: 31]
• 原级：修饰名词，不需要进行比较。
• Comparative: Used to compare two things.[cite: 31]
• 比较级：用于比较两个事物。
• Superlative: Used to compare three or more things / which of the noun in has the greatest degree of the quality mentioned.[cite: 31]
• 最高级：用于比较三个或以上的事物，表示提到的名词中具有最大程度的某种特质。
• Irregular Forms: Irregular changes in forms.[cite: 31]
• 不规则形式：形式上的不规则变化。

Examples (例句)[cite: 31]:
• Positive: Earth is large (Adjective). / John is a friendly person (Adverb).
• Comparative: Earth is larger than Mars (Adjective). / This mattress is more uncomfortable than the couch. / John is a friendlier person than Bob (Adverb).
• Superlative: Saturn is the largest planet among the three planets (Adjective). / This mattress is the most uncomfortable thing I’ve ever slept on. / John is the friendliest person I’ve ever met (Adverb).

Practice (练习)[cite: 31]:
1. This book is more interesting than the one I read last week. (interesting / more interesting / most interesting)
2. Of the three sisters, Maria is the tallest. (tall / taller / tallest)
3. This puzzle is easier than the last one. (easy / easier / easiest)
4. This is the most comfortable hotel we’ve stayed in. (comfortable / more comfortable / most comfortable)
Definition: In a double negative, two negative words are used when one is sufficient. Avoid double negative in both writing and speaking.[cite: 31]
定义：在一个双重否定句中，当一个否定词就足够时却使用了两个否定词。在书写和口语中都要避免双重否定。
Negative Words (常见否定词)[cite: 31]:
no, but, never, nothing, not, none, hardly, scarcely.

Examples & Corrections (错误示例与纠正)[cite: 31]:
Incorrect: We don’t have no library books.
Correction: We have no library books. OR We don’t have any library books.

Incorrect: Kareena wanted tickets, but there weren't none left.
Correction: Kareena wanted tickets, but there weren’t any left.

Definition & Rules:[cite: 31]
定义与规则：
• The first word in a sentence.
• 句子的首字母。
• Proper noun and proper adjective. Common nouns are not capitalized.
• 专有名词和专有形容词。普通名词不大写。
• Names of organizations, teams, businesses, institutions, and government bodies.
• 组织、团队、企业、机构和政府部门的名称。
• Historical events, periods, special events, and calendar items.
• 历史事件、历史时期、特殊事件和日历项目（如星期、月份）。
• Nationalism, titles.
• 民族/国籍、头衔。
• After a colon - if the sentence is an independent clause.
• 冒号之后——如果该句子是一个独立的子句。

Use of the Period (句号的使用):
• In declarative sentences (e.g., The stone is large and smooth.)
• In imperative sentences (e.g., Open the window about three inches.)
• Abbreviations (e.g., Mr. Quiggle, Mrs. Hsu)

Use of the Question Mark (问号的使用):
• In an interrogative sentence (e.g., What is your phone number?)

Use of the Exclamation Mark (感叹号的使用):
• In an exclamatory sentence (e.g., What a remarkable pass!)
• In an imperative sentence with emphasis (e.g., Don’t let go of the wheel!)
• An interjection (e.g., Ow! That bug bite hurts.)
